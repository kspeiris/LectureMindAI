Risk and Uncertainty
• Risk (Known Events)
• Risk is something we know might happen, and we can plan
for it.
• Potential event
• Example: Team member resigns during development
• Uncertainty (Unknown Events)
• Uncertainty is when we don’t even know what might
happen or how to prepare.
• completely unpredictable or immeasurable.
• Example: A sudden new law that affects software usage
(GDPR)

Steps to conduct the feasibility study
1. Describe the business problem or opportunity.
2. Document the business requirement for a
solution.
3. Identify all of the alternative solutions available.
4. Review each solution to determine its feasibility.
5. List any risks and issues with each solution.
6. Choose a preferred solution.
7. Document the result.
Madusanka Nipunajith - CSE 4032
Steps to conduct the feasibility study
1. Describe the business problem or opportunity.
• Website crashes when more than 100 users access it simultaneously.
2. Document the business requirement for a solution.
• System must handle 500 concurrent users without performance issues.
3. Identify all of the alternative solutions available.
• Upgrade current server, move to cloud hosting, or implement load balancing.
4. Review each solution to determine its feasibility.
• Cloud hosting can scale automatically to meet traffic demands with minimal configuration.
5. List any risks and issues with each solution.
• Cloud migration might cause 4-hour downtime; staff needs training on new platform.
6. Choose a preferred solution.
• Select AWS cloud hosting for its scalability and established migration tools.
7. Document the result.
• AWS cloud implementation will take 3 weeks and cost $5,000 for the migration.

Business Case vs. Project Charter
• Simple Analogy
• Business Case: Should we do this project and why? (If yes, Project Charter)
• Project Charter: We are doing this project. Here’s how and who’s responsible
• Business Case (Hospital System)
• The hospital needs a new patient record system to reduce paperwork, improve
accuracy, and save 20% in admin costs. The investment is LKR 4M, with an
expected ROI of 35% in 2 years.
• Project Charter (Hospital System)
• This project will develop and deploy a new patient record system. The project
manager is Madusanka, and the project will include features A, B, and C. It must
be delivered in 6 months under an LKR 4M budget.
